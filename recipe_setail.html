Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani)

45 minutes North Indian Medium 450 calories

This creamy, spiced tomato and butter sauce with tender chicken pieces is one of India's most beloved dishes. Our authentic recipe balances rich flavors with aromatic spices.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:
  • 800g boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste
  • 1 tbsp garlic paste
  • 1 tsp salt
For the Sauce:
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 60g butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger paste
  • 2 tbsp garlic paste
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 400g tomato puree
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 250ml heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken

    In a large bowl, combine all the marinade ingredients with the chicken pieces. Mix well to coat the chicken. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, ideally overnight.

  2. Cook the Marinated Chicken

    Preheat your oven to 220°C (430°F). Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers and place them on a baking tray lined with foil. Bake for 15 minutes until slightly charred around the edges. Alternatively, you can grill the chicken or cook it in a pan until it's about 80% done.

  3. Prepare the Sauce

    Heat oil and half the butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until soft and translucent (about 5 minutes). Add the ginger and garlic paste and cook for another 2 minutes until fragrant.

  4. Add Spices and Tomatoes

    Add the ground spices (cumin, garam masala, coriander) and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant. Pour in the tomato puree and red chili powder. Simmer for about 15 minutes until the sauce thickens and the oil starts to separate.

  5. Blend the Sauce

    Remove from heat and let it cool slightly. Transfer the sauce to a blender and puree until smooth. Return the sauce to the pot.

  6. Finish the Dish

    Add the remaining butter to the sauce and stir until melted. Pour in the cream and add sugar. Stir well and bring to a simmer. Add the cooked chicken pieces and simmer for another 5-10 minutes. Add salt to taste.

  7. Serve

    Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan bread or rice.

Chef's Tips

  • For the most tender chicken, allow it to marinate overnight.
  • Using chicken thighs will result in juicier meat than chicken breast.
  • The sauce can be made ahead of time and refrigerated for up to 3 days.
  • Adjust the amount of cream and chili powder according to your preference for richness and spice level.
  • Kashmiri red chili powder will give the dish its characteristic color without making it too spicy.
